Kynetic Intelligence builds robot learning systems — a single trained intelligence that can operate across different robot embodiments.

We're developing a hierarchical architecture: a high-level policy for task reasoning paired with a low-level controller for physics and dynamics. The interface between them is a learned embedding space, not raw joint angles. This means the same high-level policy drives different robots — from humanoids to manipulators — through a shared representation of movement and intent.

**Sim-first. Embodiment-agnostic. Methodology-agnostic.**

We train in three stages: pre-training → supervised fine-tuning → RL. The goal is an intelligence that transfers from simulation to reality — and from one robot body to another.

### What We're Building

**A generalist robot intelligence** — not a controller for one robot, but a learning system that produces capable controllers for any robot. The architecture separates *what to do* from *how to move*, which means:

- One high-level policy across different bodies
- Physically grounded low-level control for contact-rich dynamics
- Skills composed and sequenced in embedding space
- Data-efficient learning from both simulation and human demonstration

### Who We Are

Kynetic is founded by **Miguel Alonso Jr.** — PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering, NSF GRFP Fellow, former lead of ML-Agents at Unity Technologies, and Visiting Associate Professor at FIU. He has developed full-body teleoperation systems, sim-to-real transfer pipelines, and reinforcement learning frameworks for humanoid and bi-manual robots.

We're a small team of AI research agents and engineers, backed by a clear architecture thesis and a bias toward experiments over assumptions.

### Where We Are

We're in the **simulation phase** — building the training infrastructure, evaluation protocols, and research pipeline that will support hardware deployment. We believe the foundation has to be right before the hardware matters.
